# Research Report: Methodist Church of Togo (Église Méthodiste du Togo - EMT)

## 1. Overview
The Methodist Church of Togo (EMT) is a prominent Protestant denomination in Togo. It is recognized as the first Christian church established in the territory that is now Togo. The church positions itself as an "Eglise citoyenne" (citizen church), deeply involved in both the spiritual and social development of the nation.

## 2. History
- **Foundation:** The history of the EMT dates back to 1843, beginning with British Methodist missionary work.
- **Milestones:**
    - It holds the distinction of being the first Christian church established in Togo.
    - The church celebrated its 170th anniversary in 2013.
    - In July 2023, the EMT celebrated its **180th anniversary** during its 11th biennial synod.

## 3. Mission and Vision
### Mission Statement
The EMT defines its mission through several key objectives:
- **Spiritual:** Bringing human beings to accept the Creator, respect creation, and discern the image of the Lord Jesus Christ in others.
- **Social:** Contributing to the construction of a more just and humane society, following the example of Christ's ministry.
- **Environmental:** Safeguarding the world's resources and recognizing the interdependence of peoples, societies, and nations.
- **Active Engagement:** Fighting against ignorance, illness, poverty, racism, and all forms of social evil.

### Vision
The church's vision is "a more human world won for Jesus Christ, in peace and united in solidarity."

### The "Citizen Church" Concept
The EMT identifies as a citizen church, committing itself to the model of the prophet Micah: practicing justice, loving mercy, and walking humbly with God.

## 4. Theology and Beliefs
- **Core Doctrine:** Confesses one God (Father, Son, and Holy Spirit) as witnessed in historical creeds and the fundamental principles of the Reformation.
- **Authority:** The Holy Scriptures are accepted as the supreme rule of faith and practice.
- **Key Beliefs:**
    - **Universal Grace:** Belief in the universality of God's grace, which saves and frees all who believe and repent.
    - **Entire Sanctification:** Teaches the possibility of entire sanctification through continuous cooperation with the Holy Spirit.
    - **Priesthood of All Believers:** Belief in the universal priesthood of all believers.
    - **Ecumenism:** Affirms its belonging to the one holy, universal, and apostolic church and seeks to deepen community and action with other Christian churches.

## 5. Organizational Structure and Leadership
### Structure
- **Supreme Body:** The **Conference** is the supreme governing body of the church.
- **Governance:** The church holds a **biennial synod** for major reflections and decisions.

### Statistics (Indicative)
- **Members:** Estimated between 45,000 and 105,000.

## 6. Key Activities and Projects
The EMT is heavily involved in diaconal work and social empowerment.

### Education and Health
- **Schools:** Operates 18 primary schools and two colleges.
- **Health:** Manages a medical center and is active in the fight against HIV/AIDS.

### Social and Economic Empowerment
- **Vocational Training:** 
    - Dyeing center for girls.
    - Courses for income generation and the production of consumer goods.
- **Finance:** Established a cooperative society and provides micro-credit loans for women.

### Strategic Projects
- **Media:** Establishment of a radio station in Aneho for education and evangelization.
- **Agriculture:** The **Centre Agropastoral d'Amoussoukopé** (10 hectares) provides training in agriculture and livestock to improve food security.
- **Youth:** **Project JAMA** (Jeunesse Alerte à la Migration pour l’Auto-emploi) aims to combat youth migration by promoting self-employment.
- **Peace and Democracy:** The **PAOET** (Projet d'Accompagnement Œcuménique pour le Togo) works toward peace, democracy, and social cohesion.
